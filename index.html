Wildlife in Australia

Exploring animal species richness and distribution across Australian states.

Animal Species Count per State in Australia

Australia is one of the worlds most diverse hotspots. About 87% of its mammals, 93% of reptiles and 94% of frogs, plus about 45% of birds, are found nowhere else on Earth.
(Animals | NSW State of the Environment, 2021).

Queensland stands out in our data as the most biodiverse state, with 38,268 distinct animal species recorded. Queensland holds around 70% of Australia’s mammals and 80% of its native birds, and more than half of its reptiles, frogs and plants, making it widely described as the nation’s most naturally diverse state.
(Biodiscovery in Queensland | Business Queensland, 2020)
